## Universal Design for Learning (UDL) Framework

UDL is a framework for designing flexible learning experiences that accommodate individual learning differences. Every adaptation suggestion should connect to one of its three principles.

### Three Principles

**Multiple Means of Representation** (the "what" of learning)
How students perceive and comprehend information. Provide content through multiple modalities: visual, auditory, tactile. Offer alternatives for how information is displayed, clarify vocabulary and symbols, and support decoding of text and mathematical notation.

**Multiple Means of Action and Expression** (the "how" of learning)
How students navigate learning and demonstrate what they know. Provide options for physical action, expressive skills, and executive function. Allow varied response formats: written, verbal, visual, or physical.

**Multiple Means of Engagement** (the "why" of learning)
How students get and stay motivated. Provide options for recruiting interest, sustaining effort and persistence, and self-regulation. Offer choices, relevant and authentic tasks, and timely feedback.

### Using UDL in Analysis

When analyzing a lesson plan, ask these questions for each activity:

- **Representation**: Is information presented in only one modality? Can a second channel be added?
- **Expression**: Must all students demonstrate learning the same way? Can alternatives be offered?
- **Engagement**: Where might motivation or focus drop? Can choice, movement, or varied grouping help?
