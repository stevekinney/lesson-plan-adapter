## Differentiation Strategy Library

Draw from these strategies as appropriate. Each strategy includes concrete implementation examples. Not every strategy applies to every lesson — select based on the teacher's learning needs profile and the specific activities in the lesson plan.

### Evidence Classification

When suggesting a strategy, classify its evidence basis as one of:

- **Research-supported**: Backed by published UDL research or peer-reviewed studies on student outcomes. Include this classification in the rationale.
- **Practitioner-established**: Widely used by experienced teachers and endorsed by professional organizations, but without specific controlled studies. Include this classification in the rationale.
- **Context-dependent**: May work well in some classrooms but has mixed evidence or depends heavily on implementation quality, student population, or classroom culture. Include this classification in the rationale and note what it depends on.

Do not present all strategies as equally validated. Teachers deserve to know the difference between "research shows this works" and "many teachers find this helpful."

### Visual and Organizational Supports (research-supported)

- **Visual supports / Graphic organizers / Color coding**: Anchor charts, annotated diagrams, color-coded steps, visual vocabulary cards, concept maps, Frayer models, three-column vocabulary charts (word, definition, sketch)
- **Graphic organizers**: T-charts, Venn diagrams, flow charts, KWL charts, cause-and-effect maps, story maps, sequence chains

### Auditory and Language Supports (research-supported)

- **Text read aloud / Audio preferred**: Text-to-speech tools, teacher read-aloud of key passages, audio-recorded instructions, paired reading, verbal repetition of written directions
- **Simplified text**: Pre-teach vocabulary before the lesson, provide glossaries with student-friendly definitions, chunk long passages into labeled sections, offer parallel texts at lower Lexile levels
- **Bilingual support**: Bilingual glossaries for key terms, allow native language discussion during partner work, cognate walls highlighting shared roots, translated key vocabulary or directions

### Physical and Manipulative Supports (research-supported)

- **Concrete manipulatives**: Physical models, real-world artifacts, role play to embody concepts, hands-on sorting activities, counters, base-ten blocks, fraction tiles

### Time and Pacing Supports (practitioner-established)

- **Extended time**: Prioritize key questions over quantity, reduce item count while maintaining rigor, provide explicit planning time before writing, allow submission at the start of the next class
- **Chunked assignments**: Numbered checkpoints with teacher sign-off, task checklists, scaffolded starters that break multi-step work into discrete steps
- **Timer / Visual pacing**: Displayed countdown timers, visual schedule posted for the block, progress indicators ("You should be on step 3 by now"), time warnings at transition points

### Expression Alternatives (practitioner-established)

- **Reduced written output**: Accept graphic organizers instead of paragraphs, allow bulleted responses, offer oral alternatives, provide fill-in-the-blank frames, accept sketch responses with labels
- **Verbal response**: Think-pair-share before writing, oral presentations, recorded audio or video responses, verbal exit tickets with teacher transcription
- **Assistive tech / Scribe**: Speech-to-text software, typed instead of handwritten responses, digital worksheets with form fields, screen readers for text-heavy content
- **Word bank / Sentence starters / Model examples**: Pre-built vocabulary walls, sentence frames ("The main idea is **_ because _**"), mentor texts, annotated exemplars showing what "good" looks like

### Engagement and Environment Supports (context-dependent)

- **Frequent breaks / Movement**: Timed brain breaks every 15-20 minutes, gallery walks, station rotations that require physical movement, stand-and-share partner activities
- **Small group**: Flexible grouping by readiness or interest, teacher-led pull-out for re-teaching, structured peer partnerships with defined roles
- **Behavior plan / Positive reinforcement / Check-ins**: Proximity cues (stand near the student), private non-verbal signals, structured check-ins at transition points, specific praise ("I noticed you...")
- **Sensory regulation / Reduced distractions**: Headphone option during independent work, fidget tools, designated quiet workspace, reduced visual clutter on worksheets and slides
- **Structured transitions**: Visual or auditory cues signaling a change ("In 2 minutes we will..."), countdown timers projected on screen, explicit transition instructions ("Close your notebook, stand up, push in your chair, move to your station")
